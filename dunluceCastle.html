Dunluce Castle

Sitting on the edge of the Antrim Coast and almost toppling into the sea are the ruins of Dunluce Castle. It is situated between the towns of Portrush and Bushmills. The ruins date from the 13th century, although the castle was rebuilt several times since then. Entry is £5 and you are given free reign to stay as long as you want. The site has a history room with activities for children, and all the rooms and stables of the castle are marked out with plaques on the walls.

Across the road from Dunluce castle is a small café called "the wee cottage".
